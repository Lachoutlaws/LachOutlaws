Overview

This project was completed during a recent university trimester. We began by identifying a real-world problem we were experiencing and designed an app as the solution.

The work ran across three stages: Stage One explored the problem space and possible solutions; Stage Two developed the chosen approach into a working prototype; and Stage Three tested the prototype and iterated based on feedback.

Research

To understand the problem space, I ran a “Five Why’s” exploration focused on why people seek products they first experienced overseas. The themes point to nostalgia, uniqueness, access barriers, and the limits of local/online options.

The Five Why’s

Travellers often look for products they discovered overseas due to nostalgia and sentimental value, unique flavour or perceived quality. These items can revive meaningful memories and tastes not found locally. People who have lived abroad may also face language friction in mainstream supermarkets and prefer familiar brands to maintain cultural habits.

They’re not widely available. The specialised or regional nature of these goods means they’re usually limited to specific shops, ethnic grocers, or niche online marketplaces, so people must actively hunt for them.

Major supermarkets prioritise broad-appeal inventory. Niche items have lower overall demand, and some require special storage, import arrangements, or complex sourcing—raising costs and reducing shelf viability.

High shipping costs, long delivery times, and patchy availability make it inconvenient. Quality/authenticity concerns, import rules, and customs fees can complicate things—plus the logistics of being home for delivery and planning ahead for perishables.

Originals often have specific flavours, ingredients, or preparation methods that substitutes can’t replicate. Emotional connection and cultural meaning also make the “real thing” irreplaceable for many buyers.
