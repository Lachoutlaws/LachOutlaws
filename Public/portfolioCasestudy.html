Overview

This is a live case study documenting how I designed and built this portfolio. You’ll be able to interact with it directly and give real-time feedback, helping shape my growth while experiencing my UX thinking first-hand.

Design question: How can I create a portfolio that stands out and actively demonstrates my UX design skills through real-world application?

Understanding the Problem

The UX industry is crowded—especially for juniors fresh out of study. Most roles prefer prior experience, so I needed a way to stand out without relying on past client work.

My approach: reimagine the portfolio itself as a live UX case study with interactive surveys and feedback loops for hiring managers—teaching myself the backend required to build and host it independently.

Live & Interactive

Built-in surveys and micro-feedback capture signal in real time.

Story / Extended

Two reading modes to respect time while rewarding deep dives.

End-to-End

Self-built front-end + backend to ship, run, and iterate quickly.

While scanning roles, “prior experience required” surfaced repeatedly. That constraint reframed the problem: if I can’t show client work, I’ll demonstrate value by building a usable product that showcases my process in public.

From reviewing strong portfolios: clear IA, scannable writing, real outcomes, and lightweight interactivity. It must be easy to navigate, respectful of time, and credible through artifacts and rationale.

Most portfolios are static and one-way. Viewers can’t interact, leave feedback, or shape iteration. That’s a missed opportunity for signal and engagement—especially for hiring managers.

Treating the portfolio as a product lets me demonstrate discovery → design → delivery → iteration in situ. It also builds a feedback loop with my target audience: hiring managers and peers.

Two reading modes; progressive disclosure; embedded prototypes; quick polls; optional long-form feedback; and visible “changelog” notes to show iteration over time.

Wireframes

To explore different ways of structuring the case study layout, I experimented with two wireframe directions.

I initially tried a full-page section scroller where each panel filled the viewport. It looked polished, but it forced a linear reading path and made it harder to scan or jump across sections. It felt designer-driven rather than user-centred—so I cut it.

  • Complex 3D animations — visually impressive, but added load and distracted from content.
  • Heavy page transitions — slowed navigation and didn’t add clarity.
  • Auto-scroll features — removed user control and could be disorienting.

Removing these kept the experience focused, performant, and respectful of time—especially for busy hiring managers.

Extended Content Mode (In Development)

The Extended Content toggle lets the reader control depth on demand. Turning it on reveals additional process notes and artifacts (with a subtle typing effect). Toggle anytime—no reloads, no interruptions.

Portfolios often swing between overloaded and too minimal. The toggle solves this by letting people choose their preferred level of detail—fast skim for Story Mode, deeper context for Extended Mode.

Survey

An embedded survey system turns every visit into a feedback loop. Quick polls and free-text inputs generate real-time signal on what’s working, what’s unclear, and what to iterate next.

Each response is stored via Firebase with:

  • Timestamp
  • Question key
  • User’s selected response (and optional comment)

This makes review immediate and actionable, aligning the portfolio with its goal: continuous, user-driven iteration.

Testing

This portfolio is a live case study and will continue to evolve. Every interaction and piece of feedback contributes to the next iteration. The goal isn’t just to refine the portfolio—it’s to refine my practice as a designer who listens, learns, and creates with purpose.

It will keep improving based on:

  • Ongoing feedback from users and hiring managers
  • New case studies and shipped project work
  • Deliberate skill growth across UI/UX, front-end development, and 2D/3D motion & interaction
