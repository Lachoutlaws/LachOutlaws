Overview
Metle is a personal project built from lived experience, designed to support emotional wellbeing through music, reflection, and intentional listening.

    • Discovery
    People already turn to music to manage their emotions to unwind, feel understood, or boost their energy. While users often form strong emotional bonds with songs and artists, they don’t always have a way to reflect on or express the meaning behind those moments. There’s a growing desire for more intentional, mindful listening instead of just tuning in passively.

    The Five Whys

    Social pressures, financial stress, and limited access to tailored support contribute to rising distress.

    Stigma, long wait times, and a lack of youth-friendly tools make it harder to seek support early.

    Music offers immediate mood regulation, a sense of belonging, and private space for reflection.

    Most platforms optimise for playtime, not wellbeing; reflection and guidance tools are minimal.

    With mindful features, music can prompt healthy habits, reflection, and help-seeking at the right moment.

    These statistics show a clear opportunity to build a platform that helps young people manage their mental health through music.

    The current streaming market is dominated by Spotify and Apple Music. While these platforms are beloved for their algorithms, branding, and features like Wrapped, they don’t directly support emotional regulation or mental health.
    CORRELATION BETWEEN
    ENERGY AND MOOD
    Russell’s Circumplex Model of Affect
    Energy and mood are two distinct emotional dimensions, but when mapped together, they give a richer picture of how someone is feeling.
    Together, they create a 2D emotional space often used in psychology (like in ), allowing users to land somewhere between extremes.
    After exploring user behavior and mental health challenges through the Five Whys and persona development, it became clear that people needed a simple and visual way to reflect on how they feel — without needing to articulate it in words.
    This led to the concept of a Mood Map: a tool that lets users place themselves on a visual spectrum of energy and emotion. It makes emotional reflection more intuitive, less intimidating, and helps the app tailor music recommendations without users needing to “explain” their mood.

    • A Music Player That Feels More Musical
    I also reimagined the music player itself. Spotify’s interface is clean, but I wanted mine to feel a little warmer. A bit more connected to the act of listening. That’s why I designed the player to visually incorporate a record/vinyl element not just as a design motif, but as a way to ground the user in the experience of music. It’s a subtle nod to physical music culture, but in a way that still fits within a digital, modern space.
